# üîé How to Review the Suggested Journals

Once the Springer Nature Journal Suggester gives you a list of journals, do the following for each:

1. Match the Journal Scope with Your Topic  
    - Read the "Aims & Scope" section.
    - Make sure the journal publishes articles similar to your research topic.
    - If your paper is about generative AI in education, for example, avoid journals focused only on pure engineering or biology.

2. Check Indexing & Ranking  
    - Confirm the journal is in Web of Science (WoS) and identify if it‚Äôs Q1 or Q2.
    - Use sites like Journal Citation Reports or ScimagoJR to verify.

3. Look at Recent Publications  
    - Skim through the latest articles.
    - Ask: Are they relevant? Are they high quality?  
    - This gives insight into the level of detail and writing style expected.

4. Evaluate Editorial and Review Process  
    - Check the acceptance rate and average time to first decision.
    - Journals with quicker review cycles are good if you‚Äôre on a deadline.

5. Open Access and APC (Article Processing Charges)  
    - See if the journal is covered by Springer‚Äôs Open Access agreement with Malaysia.
    - If not, check the APC amount and whether it fits your budget.
