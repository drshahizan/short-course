## 1. Introduction

[Connected Papers](https://www.connectedpapers.com/) is an AI-powered research discovery tool designed to help researchers explore relationships between academic papers through visual graphs. Instead of presenting research results in a traditional list format, Connected Papers creates a visual network of related publications based on citation patterns and semantic similarity.

The platform allows researchers to quickly identify foundational papers, influential studies, and emerging research directions within a specific field. By visualizing connections between research articles, Connected Papers helps scholars understand how studies relate to each other and how research topics evolve over time.

Connected Papers is particularly useful for literature exploration, identifying key papers in unfamiliar research areas, and expanding literature reviews efficiently.

### Key Features:

- **Research Graph Visualization:** Displays a visual network of related papers, allowing researchers to explore how studies connect within a research area.

- **Prior and Derivative Works:** Identifies earlier foundational papers as well as more recent studies that build upon a specific publication.

- **Rapid Literature Exploration:** Helps researchers quickly discover influential and related papers when starting a literature review.


## 2. How to Use Connected Papers: A Step-by-Step Guide

*Case Study: Exploring Research on Artificial Intelligence for Smart Villages*

### **Step 1: Access Connected Papers**

- Visit the Connected Papers website:
  - **https://www.connectedpapers.com/**
- No installation is required; the tool runs directly in a web browser.

### **Step 2: Enter a Seed Paper**

- Input a **specific paper title, DOI, or URL** into the search bar.
- Example:

  > *"Artificial Intelligence Applications in Smart Rural Development"*

- The system will use the selected paper as the starting point (seed paper).

### **Step 3: Generate the Research Graph**

- Connected Papers will create a visual graph of related studies.
- Each node represents a research paper, while connections represent relationships between studies.

### **Step 4: Explore Related Publications**

- Use the interactive graph to identify:
  - **Important earlier papers** (foundational research)
  - **Derivative works** (recent studies building on the topic)
  - **Closely related research papers**

### **Step 5: Analyze Key Papers**

- Click on a node to view details such as:
  - Paper title
  - Authors
  - Publication year
  - Abstract
  - Direct links to the full paper (if available)

### **Step 6: Expand Your Literature Review**

- Use the discovered papers to:
  - Identify influential studies
  - Expand the scope of your literature review
  - Discover research gaps and emerging trends.
