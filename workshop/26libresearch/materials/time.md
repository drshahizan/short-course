# Workshop Schedule

 <img src="https://github.com/drshahizan/short-course/blob/main/workshop/26libresearch/images/lib_research.jpeg" alt="Shahizan SLR"  height="400">


| **Time**     | **Stage** | **AI Tools & Descriptions** |
| ------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **8.30 AM**  | Registration | Participant registration and preparation |
| **9.00 AM**  | 🔍 **[Literature Discovery & Understanding](./1_ldu)** | • **Zeta Alpha** – Semantic search engine designed specifically for scientific research, enabling concept-based discovery of relevant academic papers. <br> • **Scopus AI** – AI-powered exploration of peer-reviewed literature within the Scopus database, generating topic summaries and suggesting related publications. <br> • **SciSpace** – AI tool for explaining, summarizing, and interacting with research papers, allowing users to ask questions directly about a PDF article. <br> • **Publish or Perish** – Citation analysis tool that retrieves scholarly publications from databases such as Google Scholar and calculates metrics like h-index and citation counts. <br> • **Elicit** – AI research assistant that automates literature review tasks such as finding relevant papers, extracting key findings, and summarizing research evidence. <br> • **Semantic Scholar** – AI-powered academic search engine developed by the Allen Institute for AI that highlights influential citations, key methods, and research impact. <br> • **Connected Papers** – Visual tool that generates a graph of related papers, helping researchers understand how studies are connected and identify seminal works in a field. |
| **10.00 AM** | 🧠 **[Idea Generation & Research Design](./2_idea)**           | • **Qwen** – Advanced model for logic-based academic reasoning <br> • **Kimi** – AI assistant for digesting and explaining scientific papers <br> • **Grok** – Conversational AI that simulates creative reasoning and exploratory thinking <br> • **Napkin AI** – Converts rough ideas into structured visual representations (Text-to-Visualization) <br> • **Ideamap.ai** – Visual ideation tool to expand and connect research concepts |
| **11.00 AM** | 🗺️ **[Knowledge Organization & Mind Mapping](./3_knowledge)** | • **Markmap** – Interactive markdown-based mind mapping for organizing research ideas <br> • **NotebookLM** – Google’s research notebook with contextual document analysis |
| **12.30 PM** | Break | Lunch / rest|
| **2.00 PM**  | 📊 **[Data & Result Visualization](./4_data)**                 | • **ChatGPT** – Assists in designing data narratives, interpreting results, and suggesting visual formats <br> • **Napkin AI** – Converts research concepts into diagrams or flows <br> • **Infogram** – Create engaging infographics, charts, and data dashboards |
| **3.00 PM**  | 🤖 **[Writing & Deep Research Assistance](./5_writing)**       | • **Gemini (Google)** – AI-powered research synthesis and source-aware academic writing <br> • **ChatGPT (ScholarGPT)** – Prompt-based assistance for summarizing, paraphrasing, and academic tone writing <br> • **Qwen (Thinking Mode)** – Structured academic reasoning for building arguments and framing research questions |
| **4.00 PM**  | End of Program | Closing and dismissal |
